Approval to join — PickleBallers
Open Play · new

Ask first. The host lets you in.

Hosts can now ask players to request a slot instead of taking one instantly. Here's what that looks like when you're the one asking — and how to switch it on for a play of your own.

What this is

Most plays are still open — you tap Join Game and you're in. Nothing about that changed.

But a host can now switch their play to approval. When they do, joining becomes asking: you send a request, the host looks at it, and only then do you get a slot. It's for hosts who care who turns up — a beginners' session that would be spoiled by four 4.0 players, or a regular crew with one spare slot.

You'll know before you tap, because the button says so.

How it goes

Four steps, and two of them aren't yours — the play passes to the host and back.

1
You

You find a play and the button says “Request to join”

That wording is the tell. This host wants to approve people first.

You can see everything a normal listing shows — when, where, the host, the skill level, how many slots are left. What you can't see yet is who else is playing or the group chat. Those come after you're in.

2
You

You ask, and then you wait

The button changes to Requested — waiting for the host. Your request is in; there's nothing else to do.

Notice the slots still say three. Asking doesn't hold a slot — so a queue of ten people doesn't make the play look full to everyone else, and nobody's blocked while the host thinks about it.

3
The host

The host sees you at the top of their play

They get a notification and open the play. Your request is the first thing on the screen — name, photo, and two buttons.

They can take a moment. You'll keep your place in the queue until they decide.

4
You

You're in

Approved, and the play opens up: your slot is held, the roster appears, and the group chat unlocks so you can sort out who's bringing balls.

Slots tick down — three to two. The button becomes Leave game, and from here it's an ordinary play like any other.

What the button tells you

You never have to guess which kind of play you're looking at. Read the button.

  • Join GameAn ordinary play. Tap and you're in immediately — no host, no waiting.
  • Request to joinThe host approves people. Tap to ask; you'll wait for their answer.
  • Requested — waiting for the hostYour ask is in. Nothing more to do — you'll hear back.
  • Join the waiting listFull, but still taking asks. Worth a shot — people drop out.
  • This game is men onlyYou can't ask. The host set a restriction you don't match — told upfront, not after.

The awkward bits, answered

The play's full — can I still ask?

Yes. On an approval play, a full slot list doesn't close the door: your ask joins a waiting list. If someone drops, the host can wave you in from the queue.

What if I'm declined?

You're told, and you keep no slot. It's not a ban — you can ask again. Hosts usually decline because of level or numbers, not you.

Why can't I ask at all?

The play has a restriction — women only, or a skill band — and your profile doesn't match. You're told straight away rather than being left in a queue that was never going to end well.

Will I lose my slot later?

No. Once approved you're on the roster like anyone else, with the same leaving rules as every other play.

Want this on your own play?

It's your call, every time. Any player can do it — you don't need to be an organizer, and it costs nothing.

Book a court, publish it as an Open Play, and the form asks how big you want the lobby. Right underneath that it asks whether you want to approve people. One tick box, and it starts empty — leave it and your play works the way plays have always worked.

Where to find it

  1. Book a court and choose Open play session.
  2. Set your lobby size — that's how many players can be in it.
  3. Tick “Approve players before they join”, right underneath.
  4. Carry on and post. Requests come to you.

Changed your mind? Open the play from My Games, tap Edit, and the same tick box is under Joining — shown above. Turn it on or off whenever you like.

Turning it off later? Anyone already queued stays queued — they asked under the old rule, so it's still your call on each of them rather than the switch deciding for you.

For admins

Both new abilities have a switch under Feature flags in the admin console, and one button turns them off together. They ship on.

Scope. That button only touches these two abilities. It leaves payments, fees, and email exactly as they were.
