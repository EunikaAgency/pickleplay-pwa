# PickleBallers — Launch Readiness Review
### 20 July 2026 · plain-language summary

This is a review of everything the 16 July meeting asked for, checked against what is
actually built in the app today.

A technical version of this document, with exact code references for the developers,
lives in the project folder as `docs/AUDIT-2026-07-20.md`. This version says the same
things without the jargon.

---

## The short version

The app is further along than it looks from the outside. Most of what was asked for in
the meeting is genuinely built and working — the Open Play and Events tabs, real live
listings, walk-in bookings for venue owners, the venue claiming process, coach and
organizer sign-up, and the whole social section. **None of that needs to be re-done.**

The problems that remain are mostly not missing *screens*. They are missing *rules* —
what the system does when something goes wrong, or when two people do the same thing at
once. This matches exactly what the meeting predicted: a complete-looking interface is
not the same thing as a launch-ready product.

**There are 21 items. Seven of them would cause real problems on launch day.**

Two of the biggest are waiting on business decisions from your side, not on development
work. Those are the ones most likely to delay the launch.

---

## The seven things that would hurt us on launch day

### 1. Anyone can join an organizer's Open Play session — no approval needed

The meeting was clear that joining a session should put a player on a *waiting list*,
and the host decides who gets in.

That works correctly for sessions created by ordinary players. But for sessions created
by **organizers and venue owners**, it doesn't work at all. Right now, anyone who taps
"join" is instantly confirmed, there is no limit on how many people can join, and
everyone who taps gets into the private lobby and group chat immediately.

Why this matters most: organizer and venue sessions are the ones PickleBallers actually
earns money from. The paid path is the one that's unprotected.

**Decided 20 July:** build it properly — waiting list, host approves or rejects, a real
cap on numbers, and the lobby locked to approved players only.

### 2. Player-created sessions confirm people automatically

The approval feature exists here, but it is **switched off unless the host remembers to
turn it on**. So by default, joining still auto-confirms — the thing the meeting said
must not happen.

**Decided 20 July:** switch it on by default.

### 3. Two people can book the same court at the same time

If two players book the last available slot within a moment of each other, **both
bookings can go through**. The system checks whether the slot is free, then saves the
booking — but another person can slip in between those two steps.

This is a known issue; the developers wrote a note about it in the code. It is a small,
cheap fix, and it is the single most damaging bug on the list. Nothing erodes trust in a
booking platform faster than two people turning up for the same court.

### 4. Bookings can get stuck forever, blocking a court

When a booking needs the venue owner's approval, and that owner simply never responds,
**the booking never expires.** It sits there indefinitely, and the court stays blocked
for everyone else.

There's a related quirk: the clean-up process that should expire stale bookings only
runs when a player happens to open their own bookings screen. Nothing runs on a
schedule. So a court can be silently unavailable for days.

### 5. Players are never told when a booking is rejected

If a venue owner turns down a booking request, **the player receives nothing** — no
notification, no email. They find out by checking the app, or they don't find out at all
and show up anyway.

The system also has no proper "rejected" status. A rejection is recorded the same way as
a cancellation, so the two can't be told apart in reports.

### 6. Payments only work in test mode

The payment system currently runs on a demo card number that always succeeds. That's
normal for development. The problem is what happens when it's switched to real mode:

- **Bookings** would take the payment as "pending" and never confirm. The booking just
  hangs.
- **Coach and organizer subscriptions** are worse — the system grants the person their
  paid role **even though no payment was taken.** People would get paid features free.

This is blocked on the payment decision (question 1 in the meeting's open-questions
list). Nothing can be finished here until we know whether GCash is manual,
semi-automated, or a full gateway.

### 7. Venue staff can change prices

There is a proper staff role, and it correctly blocks staff from seeing revenue reports
and financial data. That part works.

But the price controls are only hidden in the *interface* — they aren't actually blocked
underneath. A staff member with some technical knowledge could change slot pricing and
venue rates. In a venue where the front desk is a part-time or casual hire, that's a
genuine risk.

## Five more things needed before launch

### Senior and PWD discounts don't exist

There is **nothing** in the system for senior citizen or PWD rates — not a setting, not
a field, not a checkbox. Today the only way to give the discount is for staff to
manually type a different amount, which means it's never recorded as a discount and
never appears in any report.

In the Philippines the 20% senior and PWD discount is required by law, so this isn't a
convenience feature. It's a compliance issue.

**Decided 20 July:** build the complete system — venues configure their own category
rates, and both staff bookings and player self-service bookings can apply them.

### Venue owners can accidentally create duplicate venues

The *claiming* process works well — an owner can find an existing venue, claim it, and an
admin reviews the claim.

What's missing is the prompt. When an owner types a venue name that already exists in
the database, **the system doesn't notice or say anything.** There's only a static
notice on the page. So owners will keep creating duplicates of venues that are already
listed, and you'll end up cleaning them up by hand.

### Player filters reset constantly

The meeting asked for search preferences that stick when a player comes back.

The filters themselves are excellent and cover everything requested — dates, distance,
gender, skill, cost. But **nothing is remembered.** Worse, switching between the Open
Play and Events tabs actively wipes the filters back to default. Players have to set
them up again every single time.

Good news: this is a small fix. The hard part — the filters — is already built.

### The map still clumps together on mobile

The specific complaint from the meeting is unresolved. Venue pins are still drawn one by
one, so venues near each other overlap into a blob.

Some improvements were made — the map now zooms sensibly and you can slide the list up
over it — but the overlapping-pins problem itself hasn't been addressed. A full-screen
map does exist in the app, but **no button anywhere leads to it.** It's built and
unreachable.

### Coaches who pay stay invisible

A coach can sign up and pay. But their profile is created as "not yet verified" and
"not yet listed" — and **there is no way for anyone, including an admin, to change
that.** The button doesn't exist.

So a coach pays for a subscription, and their profile is never visible to players. The
sign-up page even advertises a "Verified Coach badge" that nobody can actually receive.

## Things worth fixing, but not launch-critical

- **The brand colours are copied in eight different places** in the code. This matters
  for timing: when Marvin's new design arrives, applying it means making the same change
  eight times instead of once — and the worst offenders are the venue owner screens,
  which is exactly where the new design needs to reach. **Tidying this up before the
  redesign arrives will save real time.**

- **Too many routes to the same place.** There are four different map/venue screens and
  two different booking screens. The Home "Book Court" button and the Nearby tab lead to
  the identical screen. This is the duplicate-navigation confusion the meeting raised —
  still there.

- **Missing endings to the booking story.** No-shows aren't handled at all. Refunds show
  a "processing, 5–10 business days" message but no refund actually happens. And a player
  can move a weekday booking to a busy weekend slot **without paying the price
  difference.**

- **Most notifications aren't connected.** The notification system itself works well.
  But only two events actually trigger one. Players aren't told when a booking is
  confirmed, cancelled, paid, refunded, or about to expire.

- **Every walk-in booking leaves a stray ₱0 price tag** on that time slot, which could
  make the slot free for the next person who books it.

- **Finished features nobody can reach.** The organizer Tournaments section and the
  Venue Requests screen are fully built and then hidden behind a disabled menu. Either
  turn them on or remove them — right now they're just invisible work.

## Deliberately left undone

**Court data has not been imported.** The import tool works and fully supports courts —
but the courts spreadsheet we were given is **empty.** It has column headings and no
rows.

So the database currently has **181 real venues and zero real courts.**

We could have papered over this by auto-generating fake court entries from the venue
records. **You decided on 20 July not to do that** — we're still in development, so this
stays visibly undone rather than hidden behind placeholder data. This is the right call.
It does mean booking can't be properly tested against real data until the real
spreadsheet arrives, which is on the client action list from the meeting.

## Still open from earlier reports

- The **official BIR receipt screen** for venue owners — the back end is finished, the
  screen was never built. The tracking sheet incorrectly marks this as done.
- **Collecting Open Play join fees** and tracking organizer payouts — waiting on the
  payment decision.
- **Player-created game listings show a price that the joining player doesn't actually
  pay.** It reads as though joining costs ₱350. This is a live hazard in any demo.
- **The "Events" tab shows player-created games, not organizer tournaments** — which is
  not what the name suggests. Needs a decision: move tournaments in, or rename the tab.
- **17 venue-owner features are still marked "built but not tested."** No later report
  ever confirmed them. If anyone asks whether everything is working, this is the honest
  answer — it's untested, not unbuilt.

## What we need from you

Two of the seven launch-day problems can't be finished without business decisions. These
are on the critical path — more so than any of the remaining development work.

1. **The payment decision.** Who collects the money, when PickleBallers takes its cut,
   and whether GCash is handled manually or through a proper gateway. Everything about
   payments, refunds, join fees and organizer payouts is waiting on this.

2. **The court spreadsheet.** Until it arrives, we have venues without courts, and the
   booking flow can't be tested against real data.

Everything else on this list is ours to build.

## Honest assessment of the end-of-July target

It's achievable, but only if the scope freezes now.

The remaining work is mostly small and well-understood — the double-booking fix, the
stuck-booking fix, and the missing rejection notice are each modest pieces of work with
large consequences. The Open Play approval flow and the senior/PWD pricing system are
the two substantial builds, and both are now decided and ready to start.

The real risk isn't the code. It's the two decisions above, plus the 17 owner features
that have never been tested by a human. **Testing time needs to be in the plan, not
squeezed out of the end of it.**
