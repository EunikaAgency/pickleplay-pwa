01

Platform Overview

PickleBallers is a two-sided marketplace for pickleball. One shared API feeds three surfaces — a mobile PWA, a desktop website, and the backend — so the same booking, game, and payment data drives every role.

The platform connects three main groups of people. Players find courts, book them, join or host games, and buy coaching. Venue owners list their facilities, manage courts and prices, and handle every booking that comes in. Coaches sell lessons. Two supporting roles round it out: Organizers run tournaments and recurring open-play series, and Admins / Staff keep the venue directory clean and delegate day-to-day operations.

The money model is deliberately simple: PickleBallers takes 7% on court bookings and nothing else. Coaching and organizer join-fees are unlocked by paid subscriptions — ₱499/coach, ₱999/organizer — and the partner keeps 100% of what they charge. Needs confirmation The live payment path is test-mode only today; the real gateway (PayMongo), transaction fees, and automated refunds are decided but not yet wired.

Guest-first

The app opens straight into the Home tab as a guest. Anyone can browse courts, games, and clubs; signing in is only prompted the moment you commit to an action.

One account, many roles

A single login can be a player, and also a coach, organizer, or owner. Roles add capabilities — a coach is still a full player underneath.

Role decides the home

After login the app routes you by role: players & coaches get the player app, owners get an operations dashboard, organizers get a tools hub.

Three ways to play — and why they differ

Book Live

A private court reservation. No lobby, no strangers — just you (and friends) at a court, date, and time. Any player can do it.

Open Play Partial

A public, browseable listing others can join. Today it's an interest board — tap "I'm interested," see who's in, no lobby. Planned the decided direction merges it into a full game with a real, player-capped lobby.

Game (hosted lobby) Live

A hosted game with a full lobby — named roster, invites, group chat, kick, and grace-period leave rules. You book & pay for the court up front, then host the lobby on it.

ℹ️"Play" opens on Open Play; "Events" sits beside it. Under each product tab, a second row picks the view — Discover, Joined, or Manage. "Events" is a container for competitive formats (round-robin, mini-tournament, bracketing); only the public game format is built so far, so it reads sparse by design. Planned
ℹ️The Tournament tab is currently switched off for everyone. Tournament browsing, registration, participant chat, and bracket viewing are all built, but the standalone tab is hidden pending a decision on whether tournaments live inside Play or stay separate. The screens remain reachable by deep link and from the Discover feed. Needs confirmation

Booking loop

Player books → owner approves & fulfils → player pays → confirmed. The owner sees it in their inbox, front desk, and reports; the player sees it in My bookings.

Game loop

A host books a court and opens a lobby → players join → roster fills → the host is notified "lobby full." Roster members share a group chat.

Coaching loop

Player books a session → coach accepts or declines in their inbox. To exist as a coach at all, they first subscribed and were approved by a venue owner.

Tournament loop

Organizer requests a venue → owner approves the slot → registration opens → players register → bracket runs to standings.

Findings & Clarifications

Presented collaboratively — these are areas that are evolving or awaiting a product decision, not defects. Each is something worth a quick confirmation before the next build phase.

Open Play is mid-transition Partially implemented

Today Open Play is an "I'm interested" board with no lobby; the decided direction merges it into a full game with a player-set cap (roster, invites, chat). Both models exist in the data right now, which is why the same idea can look different depending on where you tap it.

"Events" reads empty by design Needs confirmation

Inside Play, "Events" is a container for competitive formats (round-robin, mini-tournament, bracketing) — but only the public-game format is built, so it looks sparse. Confirm whether to build the other formats, or whether the standalone Tournaments area should surface here instead.

The Tournament tab is switched off Needs confirmation

Full tournament browse / register / chat / bracket screens are built and reachable by link, but the bottom-nav tab is hidden for every role pending a decision on where tournaments belong. This is a deliberate hold, not missing work.

Payments are test-mode only Planned

Checkout completes without a real charge. The gateway (PayMongo), player-borne transaction fees, refund automation (3-day free-cancel window), organizer join-fee collection, and owner settlements are all decided but not yet wired. Amounts and the 7% booking fee are live in settings; fee percentages should stay in settings, not hardcoded.

A game card can show a price you don't pay Needs confirmation

A player-hosted game shows the venue's hourly court rate — which the host already paid. Joining is free. The Discover filter handles this correctly, but the card copy can still mislead until the Open Play charging rules are finalized.

Some surfaces differ between web and PWA Needs confirmation

Multi-role registration and the deepest coach / admin dashboards live on the desktop website; the PWA is login-first and gives coaches an inbox rather than a full console. Worth confirming which surface is canonical for each role at launch.

Ineligible games are shown, not hidden Planned

The decided rule is to show a game you can't join, mark it, disable the card, and disable Join as a second guard. Currently such games can still be opened. A small piece of enforcement remains.

Profile stats are still demo data Partially implemented

Identity fields (name, avatar, skill tier, address) are real and saved; win-rate, streak, and achievements are placeholder until a player-stats backend exists.

⚑"Live-but-unapproved" watchlist. A handful of things are already running in the app without a formal sign-off: the ₱499 coach subscription (the only feature currently earning), the ₱999 organizer subscription (zero subscribers), the pricing engine, rental inventory, request-to-book, and a partner-revenue figure. Each needs a simple keep-or-cut decision — none of them block the Open Play build.
