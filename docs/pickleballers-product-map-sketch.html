Product map · boardroom sketch

PickleBallers —
how the platform works

A plain-language map of the pickleball platform for players, venue owners, and coaches — traced from the live code. Each page is one whiteboard-simple diagram you could redraw with a marker; the detail sits in the notes underneath.

Implemented — live Partially built Planned Needs confirmation
Money model: PickleBallers takes 7% on court bookings only. Coaching & organizer fees are unlocked by paid subscriptions (₱499 / ₱999); the partner keeps 100%. Live payments are test-mode for now.
One account, many roles. A login can be a player and also a coach, organizer, or owner. Role decides the home screen: player app · owner dashboard · organizer hub.

01 · Overview

The ecosystem at a glance

One shared platform feeds every role. Players, owners, and coaches are the core three; organizers and admins/staff support them.

Guest-first: the app opens on Home as a guest — browse courts, games and clubs freely; sign-in is asked only when you commit to an action.

02 · Shared entry

Register → log in → land by role

Everyone enters the same door. What differs is where “commit” takes you and which home the account’s role opens.

Live: login, session restore, saved profile & remembered onboarding. To confirm: multi-role sign-up currently lives on the website, not the app.

03 · Player

Player — five tabs, main journeys

Home · Map · Play · Social · Profile. Coaches and organizers see this too — they’re players underneath.

Three ways to play: Book a private court · Open Play a public listing others join (today an “interested” board; a real lobby is planned) · Game = a hosted lobby with roster, chat & invites.

04 · Venue owner

Venue owner — an operations console

Owners get dashboards, not the player app. Below: the console, then how a booking that needs approval flows through.

Also: phone / walk-in / Messenger bookings are entered at the front desk; staff can run the console but can’t set prices or see business-wide revenue.

05 · Coach

Coach — become one, then take bookings

A coach is a player with a paid subscription and an inbox — no separate app on mobile.

Live: subscribe, owner-approved venue badge, server-priced bookings. Planned: collecting the lesson fee in-app (today: pay the coach directly).

06 · Cross-role

How the roles hand off to each other

The roles are only useful because they connect — and notifications close every loop.

Loops: booking (player↔owner) · lesson (player↔coach) · coach application (coach→owner) · tournament (organizer→owner→players).
