2. Baseline: Where We Stood on 8 July

At the meeting, the Play experience had just been reorganised, manual reservations were working, Social had been merged, and coaching was live. Four things were explicitly unresolved, and they shaped everything discussed:

  • Open Play had no lobby — joining was a single “I’m Interested” tap.
  • No eligibility rules — a women-only or skill-restricted session could not be created or enforced.
  • No money model — nobody could charge for Open Play; coach sessions were paid off-platform.
  • No platform-wide social feed — only club-specific feeds existed.

Two smaller items were also raised for alignment: Events was reachable only through a dropdown, and venue staff could still see venue revenue on their dashboard.

The headline: all four are now resolved or delivered

In the seven days since, the team settled the four open questions and built three of the four features. Eligibility, the money model and the social feed all went from “not started” to working. Both smaller alignment items are closed. The remaining piece — the Open Play lobby — has its foundation built and is scheduled next.

4. Decisions Made Since the Meeting

These were the questions the 8 July minutes carried as open. All were settled on 14–15 July and recorded in writing. This is what unblocked the week’s build work.

The revenue model, in one line

The subscription is the licence to charge. PickleBallers takes 7% on court bookings and nothing else. An organizer who subscribes may charge a join fee for Open Play and keeps every peso of it. Coach lessons go through the app at 0% commission. On a refund, whoever cancelled bears the cost — outside a 3-day free window.

Further decisions taken on 15 July

  • Every money value is admin-configurable. Fees and subscription prices are all editable from the dashboard — nothing is fixed in the code.
  • Recurring sessions — one booking per occurrence, paid as each date nears rather than all upfront, across an eight-week horizon.
  • The ₱499 coach subscription stays, with the price made admin-configurable.
  • The pricing engine stays; only the demand-based price suggestions were removed. The underlying analytics remain.
  • Equipment rental is presented as “Coming soon” rather than hidden or removed (§7.3).
  • Request-to-book (per-venue approval) is kept as an opt-in venue setting.

5. Newly Completed Features

Each of these did not exist at the 8 July meeting.

5.1 Eligibility — who can join a session Completed

PLAYER · HOST

The meeting asked for women-only, men-only and skill-based sessions. All of it is now live. A host chooses who may join when publishing a game; the app checks it against the player’s profile and shows the outcome before the player taps in.

  • Who can play: Everyone, Men, or Women — enforced from the player’s profile.
  • Skill bands: a player outside the session’s rating range cannot join or mark interest.
  • Clear signalling: restricted sessions carry a “Women only” badge, and an ineligible player sees a locked, disabled Join button explaining why.
  • Gender is now a required field at sign-up and on the profile editor, so the rules have something to check against. Existing accounts were updated where possible.

5.2 PickleFeed — a platform-wide player newsfeed Completed

PLAYER

At the meeting a global social feed was a concept only; club feeds were the sole social surface. A full newsfeed has since been built and now sits as the first section of Social.

  • Players post, like, comment and repost.
  • Games, open play sessions and clubs can be shared into the feed as tappable cards, turning the feed into a way to fill sessions.
  • Links posted as text become clickable automatically; authors can edit or delete their own posts.
  • Social is now organised as PickleFeed · Clubs · Friends.

5.3 Organizer subscription — the licence to charge Completed

ORGANIZER · THE BUSINESS

This is the mechanism that puts the new money model into practice. A player subscribes for ₱999 / 30 days to become an organizer. Only a subscribed organizer may charge a join fee for Open Play — and they keep the whole fee. It can be cancelled at any time, and the role stays active until the current term ends.

5.4 Recurring bookings and refunds Completed

PLAYER · VENUE

Two commercial mechanics arrived together on 15 July:

  • Repeat this booking — while booking a private court hold, a player can repeat it across chosen days (weekdays, weekends, Mon/Wed/Fri, or a custom set) for a number of weeks. Each future date is held, and paid for as it approaches rather than all upfront. Weeks already taken are skipped automatically.
  • A 3-day free-cancellation window — cancel more than three days ahead and the refund is whole. The refund screen now shows the real “you paid / fee / you’ll get back” figures before the player confirms, instead of afterwards.

A demo card gate was also added, so the test payment card behaves like a real one and a wrong card is properly declined — making end-to-end demonstrations trustworthy.

5.5 Public profiles, redesigned Completed

PLAYER · COACH

Player and coach profiles now share one clean layout: a sticky top bar, the player’s name with a verified check, a stat grid, and a fixed action bar at the bottom — Book and Message for a coach, Message and Share for a player. Coach profiles gained About / Sessions / Venues tabs. Friends and suggested players are now tappable through to these profiles.

6. Improvements to Previously Presented Features

These areas existed before, and were shown at or just after the meeting. Each has been materially improved during the week — including both items the 12 July minutes raised for alignment.

6.1 Events now sits beside Open Play Improved

PLAYER

The meeting asked that Open Play and Events be visible side by side rather than hidden in a dropdown. That is now the case: they are a pair of tabs at the top of the Play screen, and Play opens on Open Play by default.

6.2 The home screen speaks the meeting’s language Improved

PLAYER

The main action now reads Open Play — the wording the meeting originally asked for — with a pickleball icon, and Find Coach was simplified to a person icon. Session cards now say “3 spots left” rather than “5 interested”, following the lobby decision: what a player needs before tapping is whether there is room.

6.3 Venue staff now see only their own job Improved

VENUE OWNER · VENUE STAFF

The 12 July minutes raised one item for alignment: staff could still see venue revenue on their dashboard. That is now closed, along with a wider tightening of the staff role.

  • Revenue is gone from the staff dashboard — both the banner and the money tiles.
  • Pricing, Social and Create game were removed from the staff sidebar.
  • Staff keep everything operational: the approval queue with Confirm/Decline, bookings, the calendar, reservations, and each venue’s daily takings.
  • The change is covered by automated tests, so it cannot quietly regress.

6.4 The owner Partners screen shows real numbers Improved

VENUE OWNER

The 12 July minutes noted that this screen displayed a “₱229,000 partner revenue” figure the platform did not actually collect. The invented figures were removed on 14 July and the screen now reports only what is real: active coaches, active organizers, and applications awaiting a decision.

7.3 Venue staff

The staff role was deliberately narrowed this week so that it matches the job. Staff keep the front desk, calendar, bookings, reservations and daily takings; they no longer reach pricing, revenue, social or game creation. Venue messages are now shared with the owner through one inbox, with read status synced across the team so two people do not answer the same player.

7.4 Organizers

Organizers gained the ability to earn. The ₱999 subscription is now the licence to charge a join fee for Open Play, with the organizer keeping the entire fee. Venue pages show a clear prompt to subscribe when a player without an active subscription applies to coach or organize, and the prompt links straight to the subscription screen.

7.5 Administrators and technical

  • Every money value is now admin-configurable — the platform fee, transaction fee, and both subscription prices are settings rather than fixed values, ready for the payment provider.
  • Branding — the PickleBallers icon is now the browser tab icon and appears in the app and console sidebars.
  • Demo and test accounts restored, with working one-click sign-in for demonstrations.
  • Content moderation — reporting of feed posts is under active development (§8).

10. New Approvals Requested

Two things emerged from the week that would benefit from a clear yes.

1. Confirm the commercial model in principle Waiting for client approval

The team settled the money model on 14 July and has built against it: subscription as the licence to charge, 7% on court bookings only, organizers keep their join fee, coach lessons at 0%. It is working in the product today. We would like this confirmed as the intended commercial direction before the remaining payment flows are connected.

2. The subscriptions that now carry the business Waiting for clarification

Two subscriptions are live and purchasable — ₱499 coach and ₱999 organizer — and they are currently the platform’s only revenue. Both prices are now admin-configurable, so they can be changed without an app update. Please confirm the price points, and whether a free trial or an introductory waiver should exist to attract the first coaches and organizers, since both must pay before they can earn.

12. Closing Summary

This was a week in which the product stopped waiting on questions. The four items the 8 July meeting left open — the lobby, eligibility, the money model, and a social feed — have all been answered, and three of the four are already working in the product. The commercial model moved from an open debate to a written rule that the app now implements.

Alongside that, both of the smaller items raised for alignment on 12 July were closed: Events is now visible beside Open Play, and venue staff no longer see the owner’s revenue. The Partners screen was corrected to show only real figures.

The remaining work is concentrated and well understood: complete the Open Play lobby changeover, and connect the two payment flows that pair with it. Both are already designed and partly built.
