# PickleBallers PWA — Progress Report
Date: June 4, 2026

Three threads of work landed today: a **court-booking flow** for players, a
full **venue-owner experience** (owners get their own version of the app), and a
**codebase cleanup** of the owner console folder. Each is summarised below, with
honest notes and follow-ups at the end.

## 1. Book a court (player-facing)

### Mobile app (PWA)

* **You can now book a court from the app.** Open any court, tap *"Book this
  court,"* and you're into a quick 3-step flow.
* **Pick your slot.** Choose the date, start time, and how long you want to play
  (1, 1.5, 2, or 3 hours).
* **See the price before you commit.** The total updates live as rate × hours,
  so you know the cost up front — and you can back out to another court if it's
  too steep.
* **Only real, priced courts.** Courts without published rates can't be booked —
  the button is disabled with a *"No rates listed"* note instead of starting a
  booking that can't be priced.
* **Review, then pay.** A clean summary (court, date, time, duration, total)
  before checkout — no surprise charges.
* **Smart court picker.** Arriving from a court page pre-selects that venue;
  otherwise search any bookable venue by name or area.

### Checkout & test mode

* **Pay at checkout.** A card step finishes the booking.
* **Test mode is built in.** While the platform is in test mode, a demo card
  (4242…) is pre-filled, a *"TEST"* banner shows, and no real money moves — the
  booking confirms instantly so the whole flow can be demoed end-to-end.
* **Live mode is ready for later.** Flip the switch off and the booking waits as
  *"Pending approval"* instead — the seam for a real payment gateway is in place.

### My bookings

* A new **"My bookings"** page in your profile lists every reservation.
* **Clear status at a glance** — Confirmed, Pending approval, or Cancelled, plus
  the amount.
* **Cancel anytime.** One tap cancels and the status updates right there.
* **Sign-in aware.** Guests who tap *"Book this court"* get the friendly sign-up
  prompt instead of a dead end.

### Behind the scenes (API)

* **Server-backed payment test mode.** The "test vs live" switch now lives on
  the server, not just one browser — so the app (and, later, the website) can all
  honor the same setting. A public settings read tells the app whether to show
  the demo card; only an admin can flip the switch.
* **Real checkout endpoint.** Ties a booking to a payment: test mode completes it
  with no charge and confirms the booking; live mode records a pending payment
  and leaves it awaiting approval.
* **New "Book courts" permission.** Booking is gated by its own capability, added
  to the admin Roles & permissions matrix and synced across app, web, and API.
* **Endpoint catalogue kept current.** The live `/lists` now includes the new
  settings and checkout routes.

### Verified

* End-to-end smoke test passed: test mode → booking confirmed (no charge); live
  mode → booking stays pending; admin toggle works; new permission shows in the
  catalogue.
* App, API, and website all build clean.

## 2. Venue-owner experience

Owners now get their own version of the app — when they open it, every main tab
is built around running their venues, not finding a game to play. Players and
guests see everything exactly as before.

### Owner home

* **A dashboard, not the player feed.** Open the app as an owner and the Home tab
  shows how your venues are doing — not nearby courts and open games you don't
  care about. Same familiar home design, owner content.
* **Money up front.** A revenue hero card shows this month's earnings across all
  your venues, with an up/down trend and a spark line, plus tiles for this week,
  today's bookings, and how many are waiting for your approval.
* **Approve bookings without digging.** An *"Awaiting approval"* list pulls
  pending bookings from every venue into one place — Confirm or Decline right on
  the home screen.
* **Everything one tap away.** Quick buttons for My venues, Bookings, Insights,
  and New venue; a preview of upcoming bookings; and your venue cards, each
  showing today's bookings and revenue at a glance.

### Owner Games tab — "Your courts"

* **About your courts, not games to join.** Two views you can flip between:
  Schedule and Games.
* **Schedule.** Pick a day and see everything happening on your courts that day —
  both paid bookings and player-organized games — in one time-ordered list,
  tagged by venue.
* **Games.** The community games scheduled at your venues, grouped
  Today / This week / Later, with skill level, time, and how many spots are
  filled. Tap any one for the details.
* **Filter by venue** in either view if you run more than one.

### All-venues Bookings & Insights

* **One inbox for every booking.** The Bookings button opens a single list of
  reservations across all your venues — filter by status (pending, confirmed,
  paid, cancelled) or by venue, and Confirm / Cancel / Mark-paid inline.
* **Insights across the whole portfolio.** Combined revenue, bookings, and
  occupancy trends with a Day/Week/Month switch and a busiest-times heatmap.
* **Compare your venues.** See revenue and occupancy venue-by-venue (who's
  pulling the numbers, who's lagging) and your top customers across all
  locations — the picture a single venue's stats can't give you.

### Managing a single venue

* **Leads with business, not setup.** The venue Overview now opens with revenue,
  bookings, and occupancy — with a revenue trend chart — instead of a
  profile-completeness checklist (that moved to the bottom).
* **Insights tab.** Per-venue analytics in tidy sections — revenue, bookings
  volume, court usage with a peak-hours heatmap, revenue by court, and top
  customers.
* **Bookings tab.** A per-venue inbox to confirm, cancel, or mark bookings paid.
* **Lightweight charts.** Charts are custom — no heavy chart library was added,
  so the app stays fast.

### Behind the scenes

* **New analytics engine.** A new server endpoint crunches each venue's bookings
  into revenue, occupancy, peak hours, per-court, and top-customer numbers — so
  all the dashboards show real data, not mock-ups.
* **Proper access control.** Three new owner permissions added across the whole
  system (manage bookings, view analytics, view venue games), so every new owner
  capability is governed from the admin Roles & permissions page.
* **Demo data loaded.** The demo owner account (Oscar Walker, 5 venues) was
  seeded with ~200 bookings and 15 games so every screen looks alive for
  walkthroughs.

## 3. Owner console — folder cleanup

The venue-owner console (`app/src/features/owner/`) had grown to 23 files all
sitting flat in one folder — about 3× bigger than any other feature. We tidied it
into clear, labelled groups so it's easy to find things and easy for the next
person to add to.

* **Everything has a home now.** The 23 loose files are sorted into four labelled
  folders: `tabs/` (the venue-detail panels), `components/` (reusable bits),
  `hooks/` (the shared data loader), and `utils/` (the number-crunching helpers).
  The 6 main screens stay up top where you'd expect them.
* **Tab panels grouped together.** All 10 venue-editor panels (Overview,
  Insights, Bookings, Listing, Location, Hours, Courts, FAQs, Reviews, Photos)
  live in one `tabs/` folder.
* **Reusable pieces marked as reusable.** The shared tiles (venue card, stat
  tile, section box, booking row, completeness meter) now sit in `components/`.
* **History was kept.** Files were moved (not retyped), so each file's project
  history carries over.
* **Nothing broke.** Every internal import was re-pointed to the new locations.
  `tsc --noEmit` ✓, `npm run build` ✓ — no stale imports, no owner-related lint
  errors. The rest of the app needed zero changes.
* **The map was updated.** `app/FILEMAP.md` now describes the new layout.
* This is **organisation only** — no behaviour change, nothing for users to
  notice.
