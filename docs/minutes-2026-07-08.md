## 1. What was discussed

The meeting set the direction for the player app and for venue operations. In
short:

**One app, several roles.** PickleBallers stays a single app serving ordinary
players, venue owners, organizers, venue staff, and coaches. Each role sees only
what is relevant to them. The app is both a booking platform and a social
platform.

**Play is the centre of the app.** The player home screen should lead with three
actions — Open Play, Book a Court, and Get a Coach. Open Play is the most common
thing a player does, so it should be one tap away and open by default. Events
(structured, organizer-run competitions) must remain visible alongside it, not
buried in a dropdown.

**Discover needs to be smarter than "nearest".** The listing of public Open Play
sessions should rank by real relevance — time, distance, skill fit, spots left,
and more — with proper filters and sorting on top.

**Venues need to run their real-world business in the app.** Owners and staff
take bookings by phone, Messenger, and walk-in. Those need to land in the app's
calendar and block the court, or the calendar lies.

**Social should be visible.** Friends were buried inside the profile. Clubs alone
is too narrow a name. Social should be a first-class section.

**Coaching is a new pillar.** Players should be able to find and hire a coach
through the app.

Several things were explicitly left open — most notably payments, eligibility
rules, and what a "lobby" actually does. Those are in section 5.

## 2. What has already been completed

All of the following is in the codebase and working.

### The Play experience — largely rebuilt

- **Home screen now has the three actions.** They ship as **Play**, **Book
  Court**, and **Find Coach**. *(Naming note: the meeting said "Open Play",
  "Book a Court", "Get a Coach". The shipped labels are shorter. Worth a quick
  yes/no from Marvin — see section 5.)*
- **The Play button goes straight to the Open Play Discover list.** No
  intermediate screen.
- **The section is titled "Play", not "Games".** User-facing wording changed from
  "game" to "play" throughout the tab.
- **Open Play and Events are NOT visible tabs — corrected 2026-07-12.** Verified
  against the running app: the section control is a dropdown (`section-dropdown`,
  a custom listbox), and Events is only revealed after tapping it. The visible
  tabs (Discover / Joined / Manage) switch the *view*, not the section. This is
  the one outcome the meeting asked to avoid. Discover does open by default.
- **Empty tabs get out of the way.** If a player has joined nothing, the "Joined"
  tab redirects to Discover rather than showing a dead end. Same for Invites.
  Empty states now offer to book a court instead of dead-ending.
- **Play and Invites are separate destinations** (they previously collided).

### The Discover relevance algorithm — built and tested

This was the biggest single piece of work. Discover no longer sorts by distance
alone. It merges venue-run sessions and player-hosted games into one ranked list
and scores each listing on five signals:

| Signal | Weight | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| Time fit | 30% | How soon, and is there time to get there |
| Proximity | 25% | Distance from the player |
| Skill fit | 20% | Does the player's rating match the session's band |
| Spots left | 15% | Filling up = more urgent |
| Social | 10% | A friend is hosting or attending |

The design deliberately handles the new-user case: if a player has no location
and no skill rating, those signals drop out and the remaining weights
re-balance, rather than producing a nonsense order. **45 automated tests cover
this and all pass.**

Also shipped alongside it:

- **Filters:** date (today / tomorrow / weekend / pick a date), skill level, play
  type, has openings, and distance.
- **Sorting:** Relevance, Start time, Distance, Spots left, Recently added.
- **A bug worth naming:** "Almost full" was sorting backwards — genuinely full
  games ranked at the *bottom* of "nearly full". Fixed.
- **A second, worse bug:** the Play tab was silently returning zero results,
  because a 10 km distance filter was on by default and invisible. Distance is
  now opt-in ("Any distance"), and every filter capable of emptying the list now
  shows in the active-filter badge.
- **Listing cards** now show skill level, distance, price, and host name — plus
  venue and court, which the meeting asked to make prominent.
- **Search** matches title, venue, location, skill level, and host name, and no
  longer clears when you switch tabs.
- The API's 50-listing cap was removed so ranking sees every upcoming play.
- Ranking, filters, and sorting were applied to the **Events** section too.

### Venue operations

- **Manual / offline reservations are live.** A dedicated owner screen records a
  booking taken outside the app: court, date, time, customer name, and amount.
  The source is tagged — **Walk-in, Phone, Messenger, Instagram, Other** — and
  payment method recorded (Cash, GCash, Transfer). The reservation blocks the
  court, appears on the calendar, prevents double-booking, and is clearly marked
  as manually entered. This covers **both** meeting items: booking on behalf of
  customers, and manual offline reservations. Gated to owners and staff of that
  venue.

### Venue staff role

- **Staff is a real, separate role** with its own permission set.
- Staff automatically inherit access to all venues owned by their owner (no
  manual assignment).
- Staff **cannot delete venues** — the option is hidden and the server blocks it.
- The Reports page is owner-only (a staff account is redirected away), and the
  owner analytics were removed from the staff *profile*.
- **BUT staff can still see venue revenue — corrected 2026-07-12.** Verified with
  a real staff account: the staff *home* screen still shows "Revenue · this month
  ₱9,400", revenue this week, and "100 venues · 899 courts". The staff role keeps
  `owner.analytics.view` and only loses `owner.reports.view`. The meeting said
  staff should not see full finances, so this item is only **partly** done.
- Staff keep the tools they need day-to-day: Front Desk, Calendar, and venue
  bookings.
- A bug where staff accounts displayed "Owner" under their name was fixed.

### Social

- **Clubs and Friends are merged into one "Social" tab.** The bottom bar stays at
  five buttons — Home, Map, Play, Social, Profile — so nothing was crowded out.
  A switch at the top of Social flips between Clubs and Friends.
- **Pending friend requests now badge the Social tab** with a live count.
  Previously a friend request fired one notification and then vanished forever.
- **A Friends section was added to the player home screen**, showing friends
  first and filling remaining space with suggested players (with an Add button).
  Player accounts only — hidden from owners, staff, and signed-out visitors.

### Coaching

- **Coach subscription is live and is the platform's revenue mechanism.** A
  player pays **₱499 for 30 days** to unlock coach functionality. Both the price
  and the duration are editable from admin settings with no app update.
- **Only actively subscribed coaches appear** in the Find Coach directory — so
  every coach a player sees is a real, paying professional.
- **Find Coach** is on the home screen and opens a searchable directory showing
  photo, specialty, rating, and hourly rate.
- **Booking a coach works**: pick a session type or plain hourly lesson, choose
  date and start time, add a note. The coach accepts or declines. The price is
  calculated on the server from the coach's own rate — it can't be tampered with
  by the app.

### Smaller fixes from the same period

- Accepting an Open Play invitation now correctly marks the player as
  "Interested"; leaving a session correctly removes it.
- Booking filter chips stay visible when a filter returns zero results, so users
  can switch back instead of getting stuck.

## 3. What is partly done

These are started but not finished. They are the honest middle ground.

**The relevance algorithm runs on the phone, not the server.** It works and it's
tested, but each app calculates its own ranking. The code is deliberately written
to be lifted onto the server unchanged, which is the planned next step. Until
then, ranking quality depends on the device having location and the player having
a skill rating.

**The algorithm covers 5 of the ~11 factors the meeting listed.** Time, distance,
skill, spots left, and friends are in. **Not yet influencing rank:** gender
eligibility, play type, price, host/organizer identity, and whether the session
is still joinable. Listing recency exists as a *sort option* but not as a ranking
signal.

**Filters cover 5 of the ~11 the meeting listed.** Date, skill, play type,
openings, and distance are in. **Missing:** women-only / men-only, free vs paid,
venue or location, public vs invitation-only, and recurring vs one-time.

**Recurring Open Play exists for organizers only.** The backend supports a
recurring series that stamps out individual sessions, and organizers have a
screen for it. Ordinary venue owners cannot yet create a repeating schedule from
the app, and editing a single occurrence vs. the whole series is not built.

**Coach booking takes no money.** The flow ends with the honest line *"Nothing is
charged now — you pay the coach once they accept."* The booking, the accept, and
the price are all real; the payment is not. There is no commission, platform fee,
or payout code on coach bookings anywhere — which is expected, because the
meeting agreed none of that had been decided.

## 4. What is still pending

Not started. Each of these is blocked on a decision, not on effort.

**Eligibility rules (women-only, men-only, skill requirements).** There is
currently **no gender or eligibility concept anywhere in the code** — not in the
data model, not in the filters, and not in the join check. A women-only session
cannot be created, and nothing would stop an ineligible player from joining one.
This was one of the clearer asks in the meeting and is the largest untouched
item.

**The Lobby.** This needs a flag raised. **Open Play currently has no lobby by
design.** It is an interest-based flow — a player taps "I'm Interested" and that
is the whole interaction. There is no holding area, no confirmed/pending/invited
roster, and no lobby chat. Lobbies and roster chat *do* exist, but only for
organizer-run Games. The meeting asked for a lobby on play sessions; the shipped
model deliberately went the other way. **Nobody should assume this is coming —
it's a fork in the road.** See section 5.

**Lobby-specific chat.** Follows the same fork. Group chat exists for game
rosters, tournaments, and clubs — so the machinery is proven and reusable — but
it is not attached to Open Play.

**The social feed.** There is no Facebook-style global feed. A per-club feed does
exist (posts, photos, comments, likes, live updates). The reaction system is
still undecided in practice as well as on paper: the club feed uses **Like**,
while Open Play uses **Interested**. The app currently does both, which is
probably not the intended end state.

**Open Play payment rules by host type.** Sessions carry a price field, but the
rule discussed in the meeting — *organizer/venue-hosted sessions may charge
joiners; player-hosted sessions don't, because the host already paid for the
court* — **is not enforced anywhere.** Nothing currently distinguishes the two
cases at payment time.

**Cart-style booking.** No code exists. It was exploratory in the meeting and
remains so.

## 5. What needs confirmation from Marvin

Ordered by how much they block development.

**1. Does Open Play have a lobby — yes or no?** *(Blocking.)*
This is the single biggest open question. The app has shipped an interest-based
Open Play with **no lobby and no chat**. The meeting minutes asked for a lobby
holding area with host, confirmed, invited, and pending players — plus a group
chat. These are two different products. Building the lobby means reworking the
Open Play join flow that was just shipped, so a decision is needed before more
work goes into either. Related sub-questions: does *joining* always create or
enter a lobby, and what does the lobby do before, during, and after the session?

**2. Should every Open Play joiner pay, and who decides?** *(Blocking payments.)*
The meeting's working answer — organizer/venue-hosted sessions can charge,
player-hosted sessions don't — needs to be confirmed before it's coded, because
it is not enforced today and the price field is currently free-form.

**3. Gender and eligibility behaviour.** *(Blocking a whole feature.)*
Confirm that hosts can restrict a session (e.g. women-only), and then decide the
harder question: should an ineligible player *see* the listing greyed out, or not
see it at all? That answer changes the Discover query, the filters, and the
ranking.

**4. Coach payment and commission.** Still entirely undecided: who pays the
coach, when payment is collected, how the coach receives it, whether the venue
gets a share, whether PickleBallers takes a commission, and the cancellation and
refund rules. The subscription (₱499/30 days) is live and is the current revenue
source — coach *session* payments are separate and unbuilt.

**5. The reaction system.** The app currently uses **Like** in club feeds and
**Interested** in Open Play. Pick one, or confirm both are intentional. This also
gates any decision on the social feed.

**6. Home screen wording.** The buttons shipped as **Play / Book Court / Find
Coach**; the meeting said **Open Play / Book a Court / Get a Coach**. Quick
confirm — a rename is a ten-minute change.

**Still open, lower urgency:** the exact Discover relevance formula (weights are
live and tunable — they can be reviewed against real usage rather than agreed in
advance); the precise line between Open Play and Events; how coaches are linked
to venues; cart-style booking scope; and the final social feed design.

**Now settled by the work done, unless Marvin disagrees:** venue staff
permissions, the placement of Friends and Social, and the rules for booking on
behalf of customers.
