# Playtomic User-Oriented Guide

## What The App Feels Like

Playtomic feels like a consumer sports app with a strong booking engine at the center.

It presents the user with a very direct mental model:

- book a court
- learn
- compete
- find a match

## Starting Out

The screenshots only show one public entry screen:

- `Login.jpg`

From there, authentication can start through:

- Google
- Facebook
- email

There is no long onboarding flow in the captured set, which makes the product feel faster to enter than the other apps.

## Booking A Court

The booking journey appears to be the most mature path in the set.

Typical path:

1. Open `Book a court`.
2. Search a location or use `Around me`.
3. Pick sport, date, and time.
4. Use advanced filters.
5. Compare venues in list or map mode.
6. Choose a venue and available time slot.

The captured booking experience is strong for search and comparison, but the final payment/confirmation stages are not shown.

## Learning And Improvement

The home feed clearly pushes players toward improvement.

Visible patterns:

- `Learn`
- `Improve your level`
- profile progression
- levelling prompt

This suggests that Playtomic wants to be more than a booking app. It also wants to become part of the player's skill development loop.

## Competition And Match Discovery

The `Compete` wizard suggests a filtered match-finding or event-finding experience.

The steps likely include:

- sport
- level
- place or distance

The result screen appears to show filtered competitive opportunities, though the full join flow is not visible.

## Community And Groups

The community side supports:

- community landing
- groups list
- create group flow
- created group state
- group chat

It looks lighter than a full social network, but strong enough to support recurring club or player circles.

## Profile And Settings

The profile side emphasizes:

- identity
- player preferences
- premium conversion
- activity history
- privacy and notification settings

The product seems designed to convert active players into premium users once they become more engaged.

## What The Product Is Best For

Playtomic looks strongest for:

- players comparing venues and time slots quickly
- users who want a clean mobile booking flow
- sports communities centered on padel, with tennis and pickleball adjacent
- premium upsell around progress, activity, or enhanced usage
