# ReClub User-Oriented Guide

## What The App Feels Like

ReClub feels like a pickleball community operating system.

Instead of centering the experience on booking courts, it centers the experience on:

- discovering communities
- joining clubs
- finding meets and competitions
- tracking status and rankings
- organizing group activity

## Getting Started

A likely new-user journey is:

1. Open `ReClub Get Started`.
2. Sign up from the front screen.
3. Enter signup details.
4. Select sports and level.
5. Land on a success state and continue into the app.

The welcome copy strongly emphasizes profile setup and club-code entry.

## Discovering The Community

The search overlay is one of the most important parts of the product.

A user can browse by tab:

- clubs
- meets
- competitions
- players
- coaches

The overlay also shows:

- location selection
- sports filter
- map filters
- community filters

This makes ReClub feel more like a networked community browser than a simple utility app.

## Joining Clubs

Club discovery appears to support:

- large public club directory
- skill-level hints
- member counts
- detailed club pages
- join flow

Once in a club, the experience becomes much richer:

- activities
- discussion
- members
- library
- chat

This suggests clubs are intended to function as persistent social containers, not just event listings.

## Meets And Competitions

The app supports both informal and formal organized play.

### Meets

Meets look like lighter-weight sessions for social or open play.

Visible patterns:

- date strip
- time-grouped list
- participant counts
- location and distance
- match or event details

### Competitions

Competitions look like more structured tournament objects with:

- registration
- details
- matches
- results
- discussion

That separation between `meet` and `competition` is one of the clearest product model signals in the ReClub set.

## Creating Things

The `Create` menu is a major part of the app.

Users can create:

- meet
- competition
- club

The club creation flow includes:

- select sport
- select community
- name/privacy
- invite friends
- enable chat
- create first activity
- success

This makes ReClub feel especially useful for organizers and community leaders.

## Social And Communication

ReClub includes:

- social feed
- inbox
- feedback chat
- club chat
- notifications

The feed appears to support community posts, media, and reactions.

## Rankings And Identity

Statistics are unusually prominent in the product.

Visible stat modes include:

- rankings
- street cred
- teammates
- opponents
- charts

This makes player identity more social and competitive than in the other screenshot sets.

## Settings And Account

The settings area covers:

- account
- language
- notifications
- payment methods
- calendars
- blocked players
- community reviews
- help
- logout

This is enough to support a mature player community app without making settings the dominant part of the experience.
