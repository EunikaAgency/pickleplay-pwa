# PlayByPoint User-Oriented Guide

## What This App Feels Like

PlayByPoint feels like a club member app first and a sports discovery app second.

It is less about broad social posting and more about helping a player:

- find a facility
- reserve time
- manage member-specific account details
- keep booking and payment information organized

## Typical New User Journey

1. Open the app and land on the welcome screen.
2. Choose a goal during onboarding.
3. Sign in or create an account.
4. Explore clubs or discovery results.
5. Open a club detail page and inspect availability.
6. Move into a booking or member-related account task.

## Finding A Club

There are at least two discovery paths:

- direct club search through `Find a club`
- broader recommendations and availability through `Discover`

The discovery experience supports:

- current location
- search by club name
- map view
- discovery filters
- favorites

This makes the product feel useful both for known destinations and for exploratory booking nearby.

## Booking A Court

The core booking pattern appears to be:

1. Find a club.
2. Open the selected club.
3. Switch to booking-related content.
4. View available dates and times.
5. Use the book sheet or schedule/calendar.

The screenshots suggest strong support for:

- browsing time slots
- viewing reservation context
- separating scheduled and unscheduled bookings
- filtering bookings by category and payment status

The actual final payment confirmation is not visible, so the captured material is strongest up to the booking-selection stage.

## Managing Your Bookings

The `Bookings` area looks like a practical utility section.

Players can review:

- scheduled bookings
- unscheduled bookings
- booking filters by payment state
- booking filters by categories

This makes the app useful not just for discovery but for after-booking organization.

## Profile And Account Management

The profile/settings side is unusually rich.

A user can manage:

- profile information
- language
- notifications
- game matches
- recordings
- orders
- memberships
- payments
- proof of residency
- family
- waivers
- friends
- help center
- app feedback

This suggests that PlayByPoint acts as a facility portal where operational account tasks matter just as much as sports discovery.

## Membership And Payment Experience

The app looks optimized for clubs that require more than casual drop-in bookings.

Visible membership and payment operations include:

- stored cards
- billing address management
- club credits
- booking passes
- club account
- membership gates
- payment history

That makes the product feel closer to a facility commerce app than a pure open-play community app.

## Competitive And Match Features

The screenshots also show:

- game matches
- closed match filters
- leaderboard
- statistics
- recordings

These features seem real but secondary compared with discovery, bookings, and account operations.

## What The Product Is Best For

PlayByPoint looks strongest for:

- clubs and facilities with memberships
- recurring bookers
- users who need account and billing self-service
- players who want club-linked operations in one app
